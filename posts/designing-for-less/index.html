Designing for less, not more

Minimal design is not the absence of decisions. It is the result of making many decisions and then refusing to display all of them at once.

In practice, that means:

  • choosing a comfortable line length
  • letting whitespace do structural work
  • using contrast sparingly
  • resisting components that call attention to themselves

A theme like still tries to make those choices feel inevitable rather than impressive.
